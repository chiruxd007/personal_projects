# Localisation and Copy Samples

Independent Nintendo Australia-style copywriting samples. These examples are fan-informed portfolio samples only and are not official Nintendo copy.

## Copy principles

- Keep the tone playful, sincere and clear.
- Make content fit the channel and audience.
- Preserve global campaign meaning while making the wording feel natural locally.
- Avoid overclaiming, spoilers or unsupported statements.
- Proofread game titles, platform wording, dates, calls to action and age/family suitability.

---

## Sample 1: Social announcement post

**Scenario:** A globally directed update needs to be deployed locally.

**Channel:** Facebook / Instagram

> Ready for your next adventure? A new update is now available for players on Nintendo Switch. Jump back in, explore what has changed and see what surprises are waiting.

**CTA:** Learn more on Nintendo eShop.

**Why this works:** It is simple, spoiler-safe and action-oriented. It creates excitement without changing the global message.

---

## Sample 2: Switch News headline and intro

**Scenario:** Switch owners need a short content update.

**Headline:** New adventures are waiting on Nintendo Switch

**Intro:**

Whether you are returning to a favourite world or starting for the first time, now is a great moment to jump in. Discover new challenges, explore at your own pace and find the little surprises that make every play session feel personal.

**CTA:** Open Nintendo eShop to learn more.

**Why this works:** Switch News should be useful and easy to scan. The copy is warm but still practical.

---

## Sample 3: Community engagement post

**Scenario:** Encourage comments while keeping moderation safe.

**Channel:** Instagram / Facebook

> Every player has a different way to explore. Are you the type to follow the main quest first, or do you get distracted by every hidden path?

**Why this works:** It invites discussion without asking for personal data, arguments or risky responses.

---

## Sample 4: Family-friendly copy

**Scenario:** Promote a game to a broad family audience.

> Looking for something fun to play together? Pick up the Joy-Con, choose your favourite character and turn your next family night into a little friendly competition.

**Why this works:** It focuses on shared play, accessibility and positive emotion.

---

## Sample 5: Proofreading example

**Before:**

> Zelda fans, its time to explore hyrule again on switch! check out the latest update now.

**After:**

> Zelda fans, it is time to explore Hyrule again on Nintendo Switch. Check out the latest update now.

**What changed:**

- Fixed grammar: `its` to `it is`
- Capitalised Hyrule correctly
- Used full platform name
- Improved tone and polish

---

## Sample 6: Localised timing note

**Scenario:** Global post needs local schedule awareness.

**Internal scheduling note:**

Global campaign copy approved. Local AU post should be scheduled after local asset confirmation and aligned with Australian audience activity windows. Check time zone, embargo timing, asset dimensions, campaign hashtag usage and moderation coverage before publishing.

## Sample 7: Short-form post variations

**Version A - playful:**

> One more level. One more race. One more secret. Your weekend on Nintendo Switch starts here.

**Version B - family-friendly:**

> Gather everyone, pick your controller and make room for a weekend of play.

**Version C - utility-focused:**

> Available now on Nintendo Switch. Visit Nintendo eShop to learn more.

**Why variations matter:** Different channels and campaign stages need different levels of energy, detail and clarity.
