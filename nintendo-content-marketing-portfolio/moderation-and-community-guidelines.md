# Moderation and Community Monitoring Approach

Independent case-study approach for monitoring Nintendo Australia-style social channels. This is not an official policy document.

## Moderation mindset

Nintendo communities include children, families, long-time fans, casual players and highly passionate gaming audiences. Moderation should protect a friendly and safe space while respecting different viewpoints and keeping communication sincere.

## Principles

- Be courteous and calm.
- Protect brand safety and community wellbeing.
- Avoid arguments or console-war engagement.
- Escalate sensitive issues instead of improvising.
- Use approved responses for campaign, product or service questions.
- Keep confidentiality around unreleased or globally directed content.
- Listen for repeated questions that can improve future content.

## Comment categories

| Category | Example | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Positive fan response | Fans sharing excitement or memories | Like/respond where appropriate |
| Product question | Availability, platform, feature or timing question | Use approved information or link to official source |
| Repeated confusion | Many users asking the same question | Flag for FAQ or follow-up post |
| Spoiler content | Story reveals or unreleased information | Hide/remove/escalate according to guidelines |
| Offensive language | Harassment, hate, abuse | Remove/escalate according to guidelines |
| Console-war bait | Arguments comparing platforms aggressively | Do not engage emotionally; moderate if disruptive |
| Spam/scam | Fake giveaways, suspicious links | Remove/report quickly |

## Example response style

### Product availability question

> Thanks for asking! Please check the official Nintendo Australia website or Nintendo eShop for the latest availability details.

### Repeated feature question

> Good question. You can find more details in the latest Nintendo eShop listing / official update here: [link].

### Positive fan memory

> We love hearing this. There is something special about returning to a favourite adventure.

### Off-topic console comparison

> We want to keep this space friendly and focused on the game/news in the post. Thanks for understanding.

## Escalation triggers

Escalate when comments involve:

- Safety concerns
- Legal or privacy issues
- Unreleased information
- Media enquiries
- High-volume negative sentiment
- Potential misinformation
- Abuse, threats or harassment
- Technical issues affecting many users

## Weekly moderation insights to capture

- Most common fan questions
- Positive sentiment themes
- Negative sentiment themes
- Confusing wording in published content
- Posts that generated unexpected reactions
- Suggested follow-up posts or Switch News updates
- Any issues requiring internal escalation

## Why this matters

Community monitoring is not only about removing bad comments. It is also a feedback loop. Fans tell the brand what they care about, what they misunderstand and what content they want next. A good Content Marketing Coordinator should capture those signals and turn them into better planning.
