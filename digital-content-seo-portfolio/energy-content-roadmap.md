# 90-Day Energy Content Roadmap

Independent case-study roadmap for a consumer energy brand blog and supporting content ecosystem.

## Strategic goal

Create purposeful, well-structured content that helps customers understand energy topics, improves organic search visibility, supports self-service actions and strengthens authority signals for both search engines and AI discovery platforms.

## Content principles

- Write for customer clarity first.
- Explain complex energy topics in plain language.
- Use structured headings, definitions, FAQs and examples.
- Connect educational content to helpful next steps.
- Build authority through SME input, source transparency, review platforms, industry discussions and useful evergreen resources.
- Measure content by engagement, assisted conversions and self-service outcomes, not only page views.

## 90-day roadmap

### Phase 1: Audit and foundations - Weeks 1 to 3

**Workstream:** Existing content audit  
**Actions:** Review top blog pages, search traffic, engagement rate, internal links, outdated information, duplicate topics and missing customer questions.  
**Output:** Prioritised refresh list and content gap map.

**Workstream:** Topic architecture  
**Actions:** Group content into customer-focused clusters: bills, usage, moving home, concessions, solar, business energy, renewable energy and energy comparison.  
**Output:** Living Energy-style topic map.

**Workstream:** Measurement setup  
**Actions:** Define GA4 content events, content groupings, CTA events, scroll events and assisted-conversion reporting.  
**Output:** Content measurement framework.

### Phase 2: Build and publish - Weeks 4 to 8

**Cluster 1: Energy bill confidence**

- How to read your electricity bill without getting lost
- Why energy bills change between seasons
- What usage charges, supply charges and tariffs mean
- How estimated reads can affect what you see on your bill

**Customer outcome:** Fewer confused customers and stronger self-service confidence.

**Cluster 2: Seasonal usage and savings**

- How to reduce electricity usage in winter without sacrificing comfort
- Summer cooling tips that do not rely only on turning the air conditioner off
- Simple appliance habits that can reduce avoidable energy waste
- Energy-saving checklist for renters and students

**Customer outcome:** Practical advice customers can act on immediately.

**Cluster 3: Moving home and life moments**

- Moving house energy checklist
- What to organise before your move-in date
- Common energy connection questions for renters
- What to check when switching providers

**Customer outcome:** More self-service completions and fewer support calls.

### Phase 3: Authority and optimisation - Weeks 9 to 12

**Authority-building content**

- SME-led explainers on energy trends
- Industry publication ideas around customer experience, renewable transition and Australian energy habits
- Review platform response strategy focused on helpful, human, transparent answers
- Community discussion monitoring to understand recurring customer pain points

**Optimisation work**

- Refresh underperforming pages using search-console and GA4 patterns.
- Add FAQs where users show question-based intent.
- Improve internal links from high-traffic explainers to self-service pages.
- Test CTA wording and placement for quote, comparison, moving home and support journeys.

## Metrics that matter

- Organic sessions by content cluster
- Engagement rate and average engagement time
- Scroll depth on long-form guides
- CTA clicks to quote, self-service or support pages
- Internal search refinements after content visits
- Assisted conversions from educational pages
- Featured snippet / AI Overview visibility observations
- AI answer citations or brand mentions across monitored queries
- Content refresh impact over 30, 60 and 90 days

## Stakeholder workflow

1. Marketing identifies priority customer problem.
2. SEO/GEO research defines search intent and structure.
3. SME reviews accuracy and nuance.
4. Digital/web team reviews UX, CTA and page structure.
5. Analytics confirms tracking requirements.
6. Content is published, measured and refreshed based on performance.
