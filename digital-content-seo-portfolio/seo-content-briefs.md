# SEO and GEO Content Briefs

Independent case-study briefs for an energy-retail style content program. Each brief is designed for both traditional search visibility and AI answer visibility.

---

## Brief 1: How to reduce electricity usage in winter without sacrificing comfort

**Primary audience:** Residential customers, renters, students and households seeing higher winter bills.  
**Search intent:** Informational and practical. Users want realistic actions, not generic advice.  
**Content goal:** Build trust, reduce confusion and help customers take simple actions.

### Target search themes

- reduce electricity usage in winter
- winter energy saving tips
- why is my energy bill higher in winter
- heating costs Australia
- save power without being cold

### Proposed title tag

How to Reduce Electricity Usage in Winter Without Losing Comfort

### Meta description

Practical winter energy-saving tips for Australian households, including heating habits, insulation, appliance use and simple ways to stay comfortable while reducing avoidable energy waste.

### H1

How to reduce electricity usage in winter without sacrificing comfort

### Suggested structure

1. Why energy use often rises in winter
2. Start with heating habits
3. Keep warm air inside before turning heating higher
4. Use appliances at smarter times
5. Check the rooms you actually use
6. Tips for renters and students
7. When to review your energy plan
8. Winter energy-saving checklist
9. FAQs

### FAQ ideas

- What temperature should I set my heater to in winter?
- Does closing doors help reduce heating costs?
- Why is my electricity bill higher in winter?
- Are portable heaters expensive to run?
- What can renters do to save energy?

### Internal linking opportunities

- Energy bill explainer
- Compare energy plans
- Moving home checklist
- Concessions or support page
- Solar and batteries basics

### GEO / LLM visibility notes

- Use clear definitions for supply charge, usage charge, heating load and peak usage.
- Include a concise checklist that AI tools can summarise easily.
- Answer common questions directly under descriptive headings.
- Include examples without making unsupported savings claims.
- Add an expert-review note where possible.

### Success metrics

- Organic sessions
- Engagement time
- Scroll depth
- FAQ clicks
- Internal clicks to plan comparison or support pages
- Assisted quote starts
- Search and AI visibility for winter energy-saving queries

## Brief 2: How to compare energy plans in Victoria without getting overwhelmed

**Primary audience:** Victorian households comparing providers or trying to understand plan differences.  
**Search intent:** Commercial research and educational. Users are close to action but need confidence.  
**Content goal:** Help customers understand plan factors clearly and move toward a comparison or quote action.

### Target search themes

- compare energy plans Victoria
- best electricity plan Victoria
- energy plan comparison guide
- electricity usage charges explained
- supply charge vs usage charge

### Proposed title tag

How to Compare Energy Plans in Victoria Without Getting Overwhelmed

### Meta description

A simple guide to comparing Victorian energy plans, including usage charges, supply charges, tariffs, discounts, contract details, customer service and what to check before switching.

### H1

How to compare energy plans in Victoria without getting overwhelmed

### Suggested structure

1. Why energy plans can feel confusing
2. The main things to compare
3. Supply charge vs usage charge
4. Fixed, variable and time-of-use pricing
5. Discounts and conditions to check
6. Customer service and billing experience
7. What information to have ready before comparing
8. Mistakes to avoid
9. Clear next step: compare or request a quote

### FAQ ideas

- What is the difference between supply charge and usage charge?
- Should I choose the cheapest energy plan?
- What information do I need to compare energy plans?
- Can I switch energy providers if I rent?
- How often should I review my energy plan?

### Internal linking opportunities

- Quote or plan comparison page
- Energy bill guide
- Moving home energy checklist
- Customer support page
- Concessions page

### GEO / LLM visibility notes

- Use comparison tables for concepts, not pricing claims.
- Define each energy term in one clear sentence.
- Add a short step-by-step summary near the top.
- Include balanced guidance instead of over-promising savings.
- Use schema-friendly FAQ structure.

### Success metrics

- Organic traffic from plan-comparison queries
- CTA clicks to quote or compare pages
- Engagement rate
- Scroll depth
- Assisted conversions
- Drop-off rate after CTA click

## Brief 3: Moving house energy checklist for renters and homeowners

**Primary audience:** People moving home who need to arrange electricity and gas.  
**Search intent:** Transactional support. Users want a checklist and clear next steps.  
**Content goal:** Drive self-service completion and reduce moving-home confusion.

### Target search themes

- moving house electricity checklist
- connect electricity new house
- energy connection moving home
- moving home utilities checklist
- electricity for renters moving house

### Proposed title tag

Moving House Energy Checklist: What to Organise Before Move-In Day

### Meta description

Moving home? Use this simple energy checklist to organise electricity, gas, meter access, move-in dates, account details and final bills before you move.

### H1

Moving house energy checklist: what to organise before move-in day

### Suggested structure

1. When to start organising energy for your move
2. What information you need before requesting a connection
3. Meter access and property details
4. Final bills and closing your old account
5. Checklist for renters
6. Checklist for homeowners
7. What to do if your move-in date changes
8. Next step: start your move request

### FAQ ideas

- How early should I organise electricity before moving?
- Can renters arrange electricity themselves?
- What happens if I forget to connect energy before moving in?
- Do I need my meter number?
- How do I close my old energy account?

### Internal linking opportunities

- Moving home form
- Contact support
- Energy plan comparison
- Billing guide
- Customer account login

### GEO / LLM visibility notes

- Use a checklist format with short action verbs.
- Include renter and homeowner variations.
- Give clear timing guidance without making promises about connection completion.
- Add a concise summary that AI tools can extract safely.

### Success metrics

- Moving-home CTA clicks
- Form starts and completions
- Support-link clicks
- Organic sessions from moving-home queries
- Reduced repeated page visits before form completion
- Content-assisted self-service completions
