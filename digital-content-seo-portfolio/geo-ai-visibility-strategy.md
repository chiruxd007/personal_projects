# GEO and AI Visibility Strategy

Independent case-study strategy for improving how an energy-retail brand is discovered, understood and cited by AI answer engines and LLM-powered search experiences.

## Goal

Make content clear, authoritative and easy for AI systems to interpret while still writing primarily for real customers.

GEO is not about tricking AI tools. It is about creating content that is accurate, structured, useful, well-sourced and connected to trusted authority signals.

## How AI discovery differs from classic SEO

Traditional SEO often focuses on ranking pages for search queries. GEO also asks:

- Can an AI model identify the brand as a trustworthy source?
- Can it understand the page structure and extract the right answer?
- Does the content answer questions directly and clearly?
- Are definitions, steps, FAQs and examples easy to cite?
- Do external authority signals support the brand’s reputation?
- Are customer reviews, awards, industry mentions and community discussions consistent with the brand’s positioning?

## Content structure for AI visibility

Each important guide should include:

1. A direct answer near the top.
2. Clear H2 and H3 headings using natural customer questions.
3. Short definitions for complex energy terms.
4. A step-by-step checklist where appropriate.
5. FAQ sections that answer questions without fluff.
6. Internal links to relevant self-service or support pages.
7. SME review notes for accuracy where possible.
8. Last-updated dates for time-sensitive content.
9. Balanced language that avoids exaggerated savings claims.
10. Consistent terminology across the website.

## Priority AI-visible topic clusters

### 1. Energy bill understanding

AI tools often answer questions such as why bills increase, what charges mean and how usage is calculated. A brand can improve visibility by publishing clear explainers around:

- supply charge vs usage charge
- estimated reads
- tariffs
- seasonal changes
- payment options
- how to read a bill

### 2. Energy saving and seasonal advice

Customers ask AI tools for practical advice. Content should be useful, safe and realistic:

- winter energy-saving tips
- summer cooling tips
- appliance energy habits
- renter-friendly energy actions
- household energy checklist

### 3. Moving home and self-service journeys

Moving customers need clear steps, not heavy marketing language:

- how to connect electricity when moving
- what details are needed
- renter vs homeowner checklist
- final bills and account closure
- what to do when move-in dates change

### 4. Energy comparison and customer confidence

AI platforms may summarise provider comparison questions. Brand content should explain concepts clearly and avoid unsupported superiority claims:

- how to compare plans
- what to check before switching
- discounts and conditions
- customer service considerations
- what makes an energy provider trustworthy

## Authority signal strategy

### Owned content

- High-quality guides on the website
- Clear blog topic clusters
- FAQ pages with consistent answers
- Helpful tools, checklists and explainers
- Structured content reviewed by SMEs

### Earned and external signals

- Industry publication contributions
- Award submissions and award pages
- Customer review platform monitoring
- Helpful responses to common community questions
- Partnerships and credible mentions
- Consistent brand descriptions across public profiles

### Community discussion signals

Monitor recurring questions from forums, social platforms and customer discussions. Use these patterns to identify content gaps, not to manipulate conversations.

Example signals:

- Customers confused about high winter bills
- Questions about moving home connections
- Concerns about plan comparison
- Confusion around tariffs and meter reads
- Interest in solar, batteries and renewable energy

## AI visibility measurement approach

Because AI visibility tools are still evolving, measurement should combine platform data, manual tracking and search observations.

### Query set tracking

Track a fixed set of customer questions monthly:

- Why is my electricity bill higher in winter?
- How do I compare energy plans in Victoria?
- What is a supply charge?
- How do I connect electricity when moving house?
- How can renters reduce energy usage?
- What should I check before switching energy providers?

### Manual visibility checks

For each query, record:

- Is the brand mentioned?
- Is the brand cited or linked?
- Which competitors or sources appear?
- What answer format is used?
- What content gaps are visible?
- Which page should be improved?

### Tool-assisted checks

Where tools are available, use SEO and AI visibility platforms to track:

- organic search visibility
- featured snippets
- AI Overview appearances
- content citations
- share of voice
- competitor content gaps
- branded sentiment patterns

## Example optimisation checklist

Before publishing an important article:

- Does the page answer the main question in the first 100 words?
- Are headings clear enough to understand without context?
- Are key terms defined simply?
- Is there a helpful checklist or step-by-step section?
- Are FAQs based on real customer questions?
- Are claims accurate and reviewable by SMEs?
- Does the page link to the right next step?
- Can GA4 measure whether users take that next step?
- Would an AI answer engine understand and safely summarise the page?

## Reporting to stakeholders

A good GEO report should explain:

- Which customer questions are most important
- Where the brand appears or does not appear
- Which pages need clearer structure
- Which external authority signals need improvement
- Which competitor content is being cited instead
- What content should be created, refreshed or better linked

## Practical mindset

The best GEO strategy is not separate from good content strategy. It is good content strategy with stronger structure, clearer authority, better measurement and awareness of how AI systems summarise information.
